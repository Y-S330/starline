Safety Management System (SMS)

Starline operates a comprehensive Safety Management System in accordance with ICAO Annex 19 and international best practices. Our SMS provides a systematic approach to managing safety risks and ensures continuous improvement of our safety performance.

SMS Components

Our Safety Management System is structured around four key components:

1. Safety Policy & Objectives

Establishing our commitment to safety through clear policies, defined responsibilities, and measurable safety objectives.

2. Safety Risk Management

Systematic identification of hazards and management of safety risks through assessment and mitigation strategies.

3. Safety Assurance

Continuous monitoring and evaluation of safety performance to ensure our SMS remains effective and responsive.

4. Safety Promotion

Building and sustaining a positive safety culture through training, communication, and employee engagement.

SMS Benefits

  • Proactive identification and mitigation of safety risks
  • Enhanced safety culture and employee engagement
  • Improved operational efficiency and decision-making
  • Compliance with international regulations
  • Continuous improvement in safety performance

Safety Policy

At Starline, safety is our highest priority. This policy reflects our commitment to achieving the highest standards of aviation safety in all aspects of our operations.

We are committed to:

  • Promoting a Just Culture that encourages open safety reporting
  • Continuous improvement of safety performance
  • Providing adequate resources to support safety operations

Management Commitment

Employee Responsibility

Safety is the responsibility of every employee. All personnel are expected to:

  • Follow established safety procedures and regulations
  • Report safety concerns without fear of reprisal

  • Contribute to our positive safety culture

Just Culture

We promote a Just Culture that distinguishes between acceptable and unacceptable behavior. Our environment encourages open reporting while maintaining accountability for deliberate or reckless actions.

Safety Risk Management (SRM)

Starline applies a structured Safety Risk Management process to systematically identify hazards, assess risks, and implement effective mitigation measures.

Hazard Identification

Hazards are identified through multiple channels:

  • Voluntary and confidential reporting systems
  • Internal audits and safety inspections
  • Operational data monitoring and analysis
  • Safety surveys and employee feedback
  • Investigation of incidents and accidents
  • Normal operations monitoring

Risk Assessment

All identified hazards are systematically assessed using:

  • Risk severity levels (catastrophic to negligible)
  • Likelihood probability (frequent to extremely improbable)
  • Risk matrix evaluation
  • Risk tolerability criteria

Risk Matrix Approach

We use a standard 5x5 risk matrix to categorize risks as:

  • High Risk: Immediate action required
  • Medium Risk: Mitigation plan developed and monitored
  • Low Risk: Managed through routine procedures

Risk Mitigation

Risks are controlled through a hierarchy of mitigation strategies:

  • Engineering controls and technical solutions
  • Updated standard operating procedures
  • Enhanced training programs
  • Administrative controls and supervision
  • Operational limitations where required

Documentation and Tracking

All safety risks are documented in our Safety Risk Register and tracked through to closure, ensuring accountability and effectiveness of mitigation measures.

Safety Assurance

Safety Assurance ensures our Safety Management System remains effective through continuous monitoring, evaluation, and improvement activities.

Safety Performance Monitoring

We continuously monitor our operations through:

  • Flight operations quality assurance (FOQA)
  • Line operations safety audits (LOSA)
  • Ground operations safety monitoring
  • Maintenance reliability monitoring
  • Safety performance indicators (SPIs)

Safety Performance Indicators (SPIs)

We track key metrics including:

  • Flight safety events and occurrences
  • Safety report submission rates
  • Ground damage incidents
  • Safety action closure rates
  • Training compliance rates
  • Audit findings and corrective actions

Audits and Inspections

Regular safety audits include:

  • Internal safety audits of all departments
  • External regulatory audits and inspections
  • Safety culture assessments
  • Compliance monitoring activities

Incident and Accident Investigation

We conduct thorough investigations to:

  • Identify root causes and contributing factors
  • Develop effective corrective actions
  • Share lessons learned across the organization
  • Prevent recurrence of similar events

Management of Change

All operational changes are evaluated for safety implications before implementation, ensuring risks are identified and managed proactively.

Management Review

Senior management regularly reviews safety performance data, audit results, and safety trends to ensure resources are appropriately allocated and the SMS remains effective.

Safety Promotion

Safety Promotion builds and sustains a positive safety culture through comprehensive training programs, effective communication, and active employee participation.

Safety Training Programs

We provide comprehensive safety training including:

  • Role-specific safety training programs
  • Human factors and crew resource management
  • Just Culture and safety reporting training

Training Philosophy

Our training programs are designed to be practical, engaging, and relevant to daily operations. We use scenario-based learning and real case studies to enhance understanding and retention.

Safety Communication

We promote safety awareness through multiple channels:

  • Regular safety bulletins and newsletters
  • Departmental safety meetings
  • Lessons-learned briefings
  • Safety campaigns and awareness programs
  • Safety notice boards and digital displays
  • Safety management system portal

Safety Culture Initiatives

We actively build our safety culture through:

  • Open and transparent safety communication
  • Recognition of positive safety behaviors
  • Employee safety suggestion programs
  • Safety leadership development
  • Cross-functional safety committees

Safety Reporting Culture

We encourage proactive safety reporting by:

Continuous Improvement

Safety promotion activities are regularly evaluated and improved based on feedback, safety culture surveys, and organizational needs.
